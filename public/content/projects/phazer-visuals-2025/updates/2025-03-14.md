## Cloverpalooza — Birthday Extravaganza

On March 14th I performed live visuals at **Cloverpalooza**, a St. Patrick's Day-adjacent birthday extravaganza celebrating Roman, Lee, and Amber at **Mortimers**. It was $10 at the door with half the proceeds going to charity, and the room was packed for a stacked night of music.

The lineup featured **Ruca**, **Saint DK**, **Ghost Channels**, **Michael Link**, and **Guacaromie** across the night, with **Edison** slotted to close it all out as headliner.

## Kinect v2 Point Cloud Experiments

This was one of my first real tests of the **Xbox Kinect v2** as a live performance tool. I rigged the depth camera to capture the DJs on stage and translate that data into real-time point clouds woven into the visuals — seeing performers rendered as floating geometry pulsing to the music was exactly the effect I'd been chasing, and Cloverpalooza was the first time it really clicked in a live setting.

## How the Night Ended

Just before Edison was set to close, a fight broke out on the street outside the venue. Police used tear gas to break it up, and the smoke drifted inside and filled the room — clearing out the crowd before the headliner could go on. It was a sudden, chaotic end to what had been a fantastic night, but honestly it felt appropriately unhinged for a birthday extravaganza. Still a blast from start to finish.
